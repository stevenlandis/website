SHRIMP ETOUFFÉE

Paul Prudomme

Seasoning Mix:

2 teaspoons salt

2 teaspoons cayenne

1 teaspoon white pepper

1 teaspoon black pepper

1 teaspoon dried basil

1/2 teaspoon dried thyme

Etoufee 7 TBS oil

3/4 cup flour

1/4 cup onions, chopped

1/4 cup celery

1/4 cup green bell pepper

2 cups chicken stock

1 stick butter

2 pounds shrimp

1 cup green onions

1 stick butter

Cooked rice

Heat 7 tablespoons oil in a heavy hot skillet. Mix in the flour and whisk until smooth. Continue cooking the flour until it turns dark red. Remove the skillet from the heat, and stir in the chopped onions, celery, bell pepper, and 1 tablespoon of the seasoning mix. Return to the heat and cook for about 5 minutes.

Have the chicken stock to boil, and gradually add it to the roux. When roux is blended, set aside.

In a 4-quart pan, melt 1 stick of butter. Add the cleaned shrimp and the green onions. Sauté 1 minute and add the second stick of butter. Also add the stock mixture and the remaining cup of chicken stock. Stir 4 to 6 minutes; add remaining seasoning mix. Stir well. Serve immediately.

Serve on a heated plate using about 3/4 cup etouffée placed beside 1/2 cup cooked rice per person.
