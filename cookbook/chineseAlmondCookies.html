Chinese Almond Cookies

These come close to almond cookies we buy in Chinese bakeries.

Preheat oven to 350º.

2 1/2 cups sifted flour

3/4 cup sugar

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon baking powder

3/4 cup butter or margarine

1 egg

1 teaspoon almond extract

36 whole blanched almonds

1 egg yolk

Sift flour with sugar, salt, and baking powder into a large bowl. Using a pastry blender, cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse cornmeal.

Beat the egg with 2 tablespoons water and the almond extract. Add to the flour mixture, mixing with a fork until dough leaves the sides of the bowl.

On a lightly floured surface, knead the dough until smooth. Wrap in waxed paper, refrigerate 1 hour.

Form dough into 1 inch balls. Place 3 inches apart, on an ungreased cookie sheet. With the palm of your hand, flatten each cookie to a circle 1/4 inch thick; press an almond into the center of each cookie.

Combine the egg yolk with 1 tablespoon water and brush on each cookie.

Bake cookies for 20 to 25 minutes or until golden brown. Remove to a wire rack and let cool.

Makes about 3 dozen.
